Peanut Butter Cup Tart with Chocolate Cookie Crust

Description:

Who doesn't love a peanut butter cup? This is a classic flavor but upgraded to an "adult" version.

Ingredients:

Chocolate Cookie Crust
  • 1 1/2 cups chocolate wafer cookie crumbs (Oreos are my fav!)
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 6 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
Peanut Butter Filling
  • 1 1/4 cups chunky peanut butter
  • 5 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup confectioners' sugar
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp whole milk, or more as needed
  • 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
Chocolate Ganache Topping
  • 5 oz good-quality semisweet chocolate, roughly chopped.
  • 1/3 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 1/2 tbsp light corn syrup
  • 3/4 cup chopped roasted, salted, for garnish

Steps:

Crust
  1. Preheat the oven to 350F
  2. To make the crust, in a medium bowl, mix the chocolate wafer cookie crumbs and salt.
  3. Add the melted butter and mix until the cookie crumbs are well coated.
  4. Sprinkle the cookie crumb mixture in a 9-inch tart pan, with a little extra around the sides.
  5. Press the crumbs on the bottom and sides of the pan using the heel of your hand or a tart tamper
  6. Make sure the crust is even in thickness as possible
  7. Blind bake the crust for 8-10 minutes, then place it on a wire rack and allow it to cool completely. (Blind bake means to partially cook a pie or tart crust that has a filling that is cooked for only a short period of time or not at all
Peanut Butter Filling
  1. in a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, beat the peanut butter and softened butter until well mixed
  2. Add the confectioners' sugar, salt, and nutmeg and mix to combine
  3. Next, add the milk and vanilla extract and beat until smooth, about 1 minute.
  4. Fill the cooled crust with the peanut butter filling, leveling it with an offset spatula
  5. The crust should extend above the filling slightly to leave space for the chocolate ganache topping.
Chocolate Ganache Topping
  1. Melt the chocolate in a heatproof bowl set over a pan of simmering water or a double boiler.
  2. Whisk in the heavy cream until smooth
  3. Add the butter and corn syrup and whisk until the butter is melted and the chocolate mixture is smooth.
  4. Pour the chocolate mixture on top of the tart, moving the tart in a tilting motion so the chocolate runs to the sides and covers the entire top of the tart.
  5. Garnish the edges of the tart with the chopped peanuts.
  6. Refrigerate the tart for at least 1 hour and serve chilled
  7. The tart can be made 1 day ahead and stored, covered, in the refrigerator
